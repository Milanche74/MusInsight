Introduction to epoch

After the turbulent political turmoil and the struggle for freedom from the land of the XVIII century, the ideal of freedom appeared in the field of art as well and tore artistic creation from the canons and solid forms given by classicism. For classicism, for aesthetic meanings, the perfect composition of ideas and forms applies, while romanticism goes beyond formulas in its size and quality. Romantics turn to subjectivism in expression; the goal is to achieve the personal expression of the unfettered limitation of previous epochs. The artist of the new period is inspired by the mystique of distant, exotic landscapes, but also by the mystique and archaicness that comes from folklore. We will look at the changes they inherited from several angles:

  • Structure
  • Harmony
  • Interpretation

Macrostructure

Freedom as the main ideal of the epoch brought a significant number of novelties in the field of structuring a musical work, both in terms of shaping larger wholes, as well as on the micro level. The construction of smaller structures was guided to a greater extent by the evolutionary principle, while in classicism the architectural principle was the primacy. The result of this change was musical sentences and fragments of 'irregular' proportions (sentence structures with an odd number of bars) which exuded numerous changes of character and dynamics in a short time interval.

Amongst many possible principles of constructing smaller structures in comsposing, the most common are the architectural and evolutionary principle. Architectural principle creates sentence structures in which we can single out even smaller building blocks (eg if the sentence has repeated two-stroke and less development with cadence - n + n + 2n). The evolutionary principle creates equal sentences, and it is the favorite a means of the romantic because it is created ‘spontaneously’ - not calculated.

New musical forms bring much more obvious changes. Musical works are becoming more and more extensive, so larger wholes fit everything more creative ways. Impressive thematic material is now entrusted to less important parts of the form (bridge or final group). A frequent case is the appearance of the third (dramatic) theme in sonata form, and some forms are modeled on folk songs of different nations. There is a greater dualism between themes in bitematic forms and more work is done on their conflict and / or agreement. We'll have fun with several key new musical forms that were born in this era:

  1. Sonata form in one movement

    represents unity of sonata form and sonata cicle

  2. Symphonic poem/fairytale

    form of program music where structure serves to describe main theme of a piece

  3. Musical drama

    product of further development of opera where music аccompanies story

Harmony

Harmonious language, as can be assumed, is also inspired by greater freedoms. Consonances are conquered in the function of all intermodal ones dominant and their deputies, as well as diminished chords of non-dominant function. The use of five-tone chords is more frequent, as well as the bold use of vantonal subdominants and their substitutes. Numerous national schools have enriched the harmonic language on the model to the harmonies that exist in the tradition of folk music, so in Russian music we have a frequent mixing of major and parallel minor (horizontally, of course) better known as "peremenij lad" . There are modulations into distant tonalities through new ones types of tonality changes - chromatic modulation (assembly change and third affinity) and enharmonic modulation . New practice is the use of the Lydian and polar chords; due to the frequent accidental consonances of the bold construction and the fact that each chord was possible to be found in any tonality, the tonality headed for its disintegration. The tonal center has become a very relative thing.

Interpretation

During XIXth century, production of musical instruments quite improved leading to increase in sound quality of ensambles of that time and gave musicians chance to extend borders of their techical capability as performers. Greater musical forms led to augmentation of orchestral apparatus. Great, newly opened concert halls and opera houses standed as a places to host such magnificent, sometimes even pretentious musical events.

Romantic performer held to subjectivism in expression as his sharpest weapon, tearing off rules imposed by classical era. Articulation and dynamics marks were much more emphasized, thus increasing musical divergence. Agogic shifts were main means of expressing internal sentiment.

XIXth century gave birth to many widely recognized performers, solidifying artists as professionals.
